ABOUT AUTISM

Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is a developmental disability that can cause significant social, communication and behavioral challenges. There is often nothing about how people with ASD look that sets them apart from other people, but people with ASD may communicate, interact, behave, and learn in ways that are different from most other people. The learning, thinking, and problem-solving abilities of people with ASD can range from gifted to severely challenged. Some people with ASD need a lot of help in their daily lives; others need less.

SYMPTOMS

DIAGNOSIS

  • Social Interaction Difficulties
    People with ASD often struggle with initiating and sustaining reciprocal social interaction.
  • Communication Challenges
    Individuals with ASD may experience difficulties in communication.
  • Restricted Interests and Repetitive Behaviors
    A hallmark of ASD is the presence of restricted, repetitive, and inflexible patterns of behavior, interests, or activities.
  • Atypical Learning and Attention Patterns
    People with ASD may exhibit unique ways of learning, movement differences, or attention variations.
  • A diagnosis of ASD now includes several conditions that used to be diagnosed separately:
  • AUTISTIC DISORDER
  • PERVASIVE DEVELOPMENTAL DISORDER
  • ASPERGER SYNDROME
